Who Were the Signers

Most Famous Signers

Thomas Jefferson
Principal author of the Declaration of Independence and later the 3rd President of the United States.
John Adams
Strong advocate for independence and later the 2nd President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin
Famous inventor, diplomat, and elder statesman, admired both in America and Europe.
John Hancock
President of the Continental Congress, remembered for his large, bold signature.
Samuel Adams
Prominent organizer of resistance, including the Boston Tea Party and Committees of Correspondence.
George Wythe
Respected legal scholar and mentor to Thomas Jefferson.
Charles Carroll of Carrollton
The only Catholic signer and the last surviving signer, living until 1832.
Richard Henry Lee
Introduced the resolution calling for independence in the Continental Congress.
